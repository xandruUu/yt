# Content Strategy

Priority niches:

- AI tools.
- Technology explained.
- Science explained.
- Internet culture explained.
- Business mistakes and case studies.
- Engineering curiosities.
- Productivity and psychology.
- Educational finance with caution.

Every video should aim for original script, original structure, legal visuals, clear editing, real localization, fact-checking, and human review.

Avoid aggressive politics, sensitive breaking news, graphic crime, unlicensed sports/film/anime clips, deepfakes, dangerous medical claims, miracle trading claims, and misleading money promises.

## Trend-to-idea rules

- Treat trends as signals, not scripts to copy.
- Rewrite each trend into an original angle with a clear first-second hook.
- Prefer explainers, business mistakes, engineering/science mechanisms, productivity, psychology, and tech/AI topics with legal visual paths.
- Down-rank ideas that require third-party clips, celebrity footage, sports highlights, music fragments, movie/anime scenes, or unverifiable claims.
- Keep generated ideas short-form friendly: one core question, one payoff, one visual metaphor, and a 30-45 second default.

## Hook-to-script rules

- Titles should be clear first, curious second, and never promise what the script cannot deliver.
- Metadata should stay short, include `#shorts`, and avoid hashtag spam.
- Scripts should start directly with the selected hook or a strong continuation of it.
- Every script line should work as a readable subtitle and include a visual suggestion.
- Numbers, dates, money, absolutes, health, finance, legal, politics and security claims should be marked for source review.
- A script can have warnings, but high-risk unresolved claims should block approval.
